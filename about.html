Crawford & Son

A neighborhood restaurant in Raleigh's historic Oakwood, rooted in family, product, and craft.

The Restaurant

Crawford & Son opened in November 2016 on Person Street in Raleigh's historic Oakwood neighborhood. The name reflects Chef Scott Crawford's commitment to family and community—honoring both the past and the people who make it all possible.

It's casual. It's honest. It's straightforward food that doesn't apologize for being straightforward. The kitchen operates from a product-driven philosophy, combining classical technique with modern sensibility. What you eat depends on what's available—what's at the market, what's in season, what speaks to the team that morning.

The Team

Behind every dish stands a team held together by what can only be described as undeniable chemistry. General Manager Kat Rider runs front-of-house with precision and warmth. Chef de Cuisine Aaron Salita leads the kitchen with the same commitment to excellence that defines every plate. Beverage Director Jordan Joseph has built a program that elevates the experience from first sip to last.

It's the kind of restaurant where everyone knows their role and executes it with quiet confidence. No ego. No theater. Just really good food and drinks, served by people who care.

Recognition

In 2018, the Triangle named Crawford & Son Restaurant of the Year. Since opening, Chef Scott Crawford has been a six-time James Beard Award semifinalist for Best Chef Southeast—recognition of the approach, the discipline, and the work that goes into every service.

Eater called it the "crown jewel" of Crawford's restaurant empire. The Infatuation labeled it a "standard-bearer for the city's upper-crust dining." But those words matter less than what happens when you walk through the door. The food is direct. The service is genuine. The experience is complete.

A Growing Restaurant Family

Crawford & Son remains the flagship—the place where it all began. But it's not alone. Chef Scott Crawford's vision has expanded across Raleigh: Jolie (2019), Brodeto, Bar Sous Terre, and Crawford Brothers Steakhouse each bring their own identity to the city's dining landscape, while sharing the same philosophy of quality, craft, and care.
