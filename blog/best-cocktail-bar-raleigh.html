Best Cocktail Bar in Raleigh

Published March 17, 2026

Jordan Joseph, our beverage director, doesn't make cocktails — he makes ideas you drink. The menu changes with the season, which means the bar at Crawford & Son is never the same conversation twice. And that's the point. The drinks are built to pair with what's good right now, what Chef Scott's excited about, what tastes true to the moment.

The Green Mile walks a line between sweet and herbal — the kind of drink that tastes like the name makes sense after the first sip. Lord of the Flies brings tropical notes and a sharper edge. Rush Hour is built for the person sitting at the bar on the way home, needing something clean and alive. Rose In Harlem does what a great cocktail does — it tastes like something specific, and you want another. These aren't throwaway names. Jordan thinks about what each drink is supposed to do in your evening and builds backward from there.

The spirit-free program isn't an afterthought. If you want a cocktail without alcohol, Jordan creates that with the same thought. The wine list is curated — VinePair has covered our program — and it's built around bottles that pair as naturally with the food as they do with good conversation. There are deep pours from bottles worth getting to know, and there are accessibility points where you can explore without commitment.

The bar itself is the best seat in the restaurant. You're sitting close enough to watch Jordan work, to understand the thought that goes into each pour. The bartenders know what they're doing — they move with the rhythm of the dining room. There's no rushing. You'll watch the night unfold while you're nursing something genuinely well-made, and you'll understand why craft cocktails at Crawford & Son feel like a reason to come back on its own.

The seasonal menu means asking Jordan what's current. Check the full menu, make a reservation, or pull up a stool at the bar. Let him show you what he's thinking about this season.
