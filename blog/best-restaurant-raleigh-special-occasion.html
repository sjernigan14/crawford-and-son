Best Restaurant in Raleigh for a Special Occasion

Published March 17, 2026

The Infatuation called it Raleigh's top anniversary pick. And they're not wrong. Walking off Person Street in historic Oakwood and into Crawford & Son, you feel the shift immediately — the candlelit tables, the low hum of conversation, the sense that something matters is happening tonight. This is where you bring someone when it needs to be perfect.

Chef Scott Crawford built this restaurant around the kind of cooking that doesn't announce itself. The Wagyu short rib arrives quietly on a white plate — braised until it falls apart, its umami running deep. The duck breast is sliced pink, its skin rendered to a copper crackle. These aren't showpieces. They're the dishes people remember years later, when they're telling the story of the night they got engaged, celebrated an anniversary, marked something worth marking.

The seasonal menu shifts with what's good, what's in. In spring, you might find Manchego-herb biscuits that taste like butter and herbs did a perfect handshake. In fall, the wine list deepens — Jordan Joseph, our beverage director, has curated something that pairs as well with the food as it does with conversation. There's craft to it, but no pretension. Just thoughtful pours that taste like they belong.

The intimate setting works because it actually is intimate. Forty-five seats. Exposed brick. No view of anyone else's phone. You're sitting across from someone, the candlelight doing what candlelight does, and you're tasting something genuinely good. The kitchen isn't trying to be the loudest voice in the room — Chef Scott lets the food speak. A perfectly cooked piece of fish. A vegetable that tastes like itself, elevated.

These are the details that separate a nice dinner from a night you talk about. The butter on the bread. The temperature of the plate. The way Jordan's cocktails — The Green Mile, Lord of the Flies, something he invented just for you — arrive exactly when you need them. The team moving around the dining room with attention but not anxiety.

Historic Oakwood has grown into a neighborhood where people want to be. Person Street feels lived-in, walkable, real. After dinner, you can wander. There are galleries, coffee shops, other people moving through the same evening you're moving through. It doesn't feel like you drove downtown to a restaurant and then drove back home. It feels like you went somewhere that belongs to the city.

When you're planning something that matters — the proposal, the milestone, the reason you want to feel like you tried — Crawford & Son is where Raleigh comes. Not because it's trying to be the fanciest or the loudest, but because it's genuinely, reliably good. Because Chef Scott and his team care about what happens at that table as much as you do.

Make a reservation. Check the seasonal menu. Walk in on a night that matters.
