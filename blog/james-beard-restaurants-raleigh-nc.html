James Beard Recognition at Crawford & Son

March 2026 • Stories

The James Beard Award is not something a restaurant chases. If you're in the kitchen thinking about plaques and rankings, you're already lost.

At Crawford & Son, Chef Scott Crawford and his team are thinking about something simpler: Is this ingredient ready? Did we cook this properly? Is this something worth eating? The work is honest. The focus is narrow. The recognition has followed.

Crawford & Son has been a James Beard Award semifinalist five times. That alone says something—not about awards, but about consistency. About a kitchen that keeps showing up, keeps caring, keeps refusing shortcuts. WRAL reported the restaurant as an Outstanding Hospitality finalist, which touches on something equally important: you can have perfect food and a cold room. Crawford & Son is neither.

What the James Beard Award actually means is harder to articulate. It's not a measure of how good the food is—some of the best food in America is made in places you've never heard of. It's more a recognition that a restaurant has built something sustained, something that matters, something that people outside its immediate neighborhood have noticed and believe in.

That recognition belongs to the whole team. Kat Rider manages hospitality with the kind of thoughtfulness that's invisible until you notice its absence—which is exactly when you know it's working. Aaron Salita runs the kitchen with the discipline of someone who understands that execution is everything. Chef Crawford writes the menu, sources the ingredients, and makes the decisions. They move together.

Raleigh's dining scene has grown. Garden & Gun took notice. The city has restaurants now that draw people from other cities. That shift didn't happen overnight. It happened because a few places—like this one, on Person Street in Oakwood—refused to be ordinary. The seminfinalist nominations document that refusal. They're not why Crawford & Son cooks the way it does. But they're evidence that the cooking matters.

There's also the sister restaurant ecosystem: the work that got built around this place, the team that expanded to other projects. That's what happens when you do something right—you earn the privilege of doing more.

The James Beard semifinalist status doesn't change what lands on your plate. It doesn't make the dining room warmer or the cocktail better. What it does is confirm what you'll discover the moment you sit down: this restaurant cares more than most.
